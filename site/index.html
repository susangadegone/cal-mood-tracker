Project overview

Cal Mood Tracker

A mobile app for people whose calendar and their mood are basically the same document. It puts wellness breaks, mood check-ins, and notes on the same schedule as meetings, so looking after yourself is one more block in the day instead of a separate thing you never get to.

iOS & Android Expo / React Native Offline-first, on-device storage

How the day actually works

The app is built around the same 7 AM–10 PM window most people actually live in, split into 30-minute slots. Everything below lives on that timeline, not in a separate menu.

7:00
Calendar

Day, week, and month views, with work kept apart from everything else

Two calendars run side by side, work in blue and personal in green, so a packed workday and a quiet evening are still visibly different things at a glance. Day view breaks the day into Morning, Work Time, and Evening.

Screenshot slot: Day view with a mix of work meetings and a personal event, showing the dual blue/green calendars.
2:00
Wellness

Five-minute activities you can actually fit into a packed afternoon

A small library of short wellness activities can be dropped straight into an open slot, the same way you'd add a meeting. The idea is to lower the bar enough that a break actually happens instead of getting pushed to tomorrow.

Screenshot slot: The wellness activity library open next to a day view, mid-drag into a 2:00 PM slot.
6:00
Mood

A five-point mood scale, logged in a couple of taps

Excellent, Good, Okay, Poor, or Terrible, with an optional note attached. Logged entries build into a history you can look back over, so a bad Tuesday reads as a data point instead of the whole week.

Excellent Good Okay Poor Terrible
Screenshot slot: The mood picker mid-selection, plus the weekly insights view showing a run of entries.
8:30
Notes

Notes that save themselves, for the thought you don't want to lose

A running place for tasks and general notes that sits apart from the calendar, for things that don't need their own event and don't need you to remember to hit save.

9:30
Stats

The week, added up

A dashboard totals events, wellness activities, mood entries, and streaks, with a mood distribution chart and weekly and monthly summaries, so patterns show up without anyone having to go digging for them.

Screenshot slot: The stats dashboard with a streak counter and mood distribution chart populated by a few weeks of real use.

Design

Pastel throughout, purple, pink, green, blue, and orange, used mostly as the mood-state colors above rather than scattered decoration. Clean, minimal layouts, modern sans-serif type, and accessible contrast even at pastel saturation.

Built with

Data lives in AsyncStorage on the device: accounts, events, mood entries, notes, settings, and stats, with no backend in the loop today.
